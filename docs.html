Introduction to JobAgentX

JobAgentX helps your team source, engage, and hire faster. Instead of digging through job boards, you describe who you're looking for and JobAgentX surfaces matching candidates, drafts outreach, and books interviews for you — so your pipeline fills itself while you focus on the conversations that matter.

This guide walks you through everything you need to get up and running: creating your first position, connecting your inbox, and launching your first search. It takes about five minutes.

Watch: Getting started with JobAgentX (2:14)
Before you start: Make sure you've confirmed your email invite. If you haven't received one, ask your workspace admin to resend it from Settings → Team.

Step 1: Create your job

Every search starts with a position. A job tells JobAgentX what role you're hiring for, where, and what "good" looks like — the agent uses this to score and rank candidates.

Open the position creator

From the sidebar, click the + next to your workspace name and choose Create New Job Position.

Describe the role

Add a title, location, and a short description. The more specific the must-haves, the sharper your matches.

Save and continue

Your new position appears under My Positions. You're ready to set up outreach.

Step 2: Set up outreach

Connect the inbox you want messages to send from, then pick a sequence. JobAgentX personalizes each message to the candidate and spaces sends out so your domain stays healthy.

  • Connect Google or Microsoft in one click — no SMTP setup.
  • Choose a starter sequence, or write your own steps.
  • Replies land back in your JobAgentX Inbox, threaded per candidate.

Step 3: Add candidates from Sourcing

Head to Sourcing and describe your ideal candidate in plain language. JobAgentX returns a ranked list with match reasons. Select the ones you want and add them straight into your outreach queue.

Quick tips with Sourcing

  • Start broad, then tighten filters once you see who's showing up.
  • Use the match explanations to refine what the agent prioritizes.
  • Unlock contact info only for candidates you actually plan to reach — it keeps credits efficient.
